Your data & privacy

Plain-language summary

What this is. Auritech is a space for reflection and contemplative practice. It is not medical or psychological care, it does not diagnose or treat any condition, and it is not a substitute for a professional.

What's collected. Your username, your intake answers, your diary entries, your session conversations, any photo you choose to share for a reading, and your progress.

How it's protected. Your password is stored only as a one-way hash. Your data is encrypted at rest and kept strictly separate from every other user's — no one else can access yours.

Who it's shared with. To generate a reply, your messages (and any photo you send) are passed to the AI provider that powers the guide. Your data is not sold and not shared with advertisers.

Your choices. You can read your entries back, stop at any time, and permanently delete your account and all your data from the Account screen.

This is a plain summary, not a legal document. A full privacy policy and consent flow should be in place before the app is opened to the public.

Before we begin

Practice with your eyes closed

Auritech can read guidance aloud and listen to your spoken replies, so you can keep your eyes closed during a session. To do that, it needs permission to use your microphone.

Nothing is recorded or stored — your voice is turned into text on your device, in the moment, and then it's gone.
